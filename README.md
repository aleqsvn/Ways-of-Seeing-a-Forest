# 🌳 Ways of Seeing a Forest
## 📄 Essay
A short reflection on attention, perception, and the quiet act of observing the world.
## 🌿 Summary
A year ago I buried biodegradable bags in the forest to test whether they truly disappear or if it is just marketing language.
The earth does not read labels. It does not believe promises. It simply processes what it is given.
When I returned, there was nothing left.
But the real observation that day was not about the experiment.
It was about attention.
## 👩🌲 Scene
In the forest I noticed a girl in a green beanie, wearing a coat and holding a stick.
She was not searching for anything specific.
She was simply exploring.
She was gently moving leaves with a stick, as if testing the surface of the forest.
There was curiosity in the way she moved through the forest.
Not goal-oriented. Not practical. Just attention.
## 🧠 Idea
Attention is not just a tool.
It is a way of being in the world.
Science does not begin with answers.
It begins with a question that does not yet know what it is looking for.
Like moving a stick through leaves.
Touching reality without forcing meaning onto it.
## ⚡ Conclusion
Each of us remains within our own perception.
But attention allows something else.
It allows a direct contact with the world.
Not through interpretation.
Not through purpose.
But through presence.
And perhaps this is where both science and experience begin.
